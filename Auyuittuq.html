Auyuittuq National Park Trail, Canada - Planning Document (2024)

I. Introduction:

Objective:

  • Embark on an exhilarating adventure through Auyuittuq National Park, Canada, from June 1, 2024, to June 10, 2024, exploring its majestic landscapes and rich cultural heritage

A. Location:

Auyuittuq National Park:

  • Situated in Nunavut, Canada
  • Home to breathtaking landscapes, including the Penny Ice Cap and Mount Thor

    • B. Transportation:

    Arrival:

  • Fly into Iqaluit Airport on June 1, 2024
  • Arrange charter flights to Pangnirtung, the gateway to Auyuittuq National Park

    • Local Transportation

  • Utilize local guides and transportation for transfers between Pangnirtung and the trailhead
  • Consider boat transfers for water sections

    • Departure:

  • Plan for departure from Iqaluit Airport on June 10, 2024

    • III. Logistics:

    A. Accommodations:

  • Camping along the trail
  • Ensure proper permits for camping within the national park

    • B. Equipment:

  • Confirm availability of camping gear locally
  • Discuss transportation or rental options for personal gear
  • Consider a local outfitter for specialized equipment

    • C. Guides and Instructors:

  • Engage local Inuit guides with expertise in the region
  • Ensure they are well-versed in the cultural significance of the area

    • IV. Safety and Health:

    A. Emergency Plan:

    1. Emergency Response:

  • Detail emergency procedures, including contact information for local authorities and medical facilities
  • Establish a meeting point for participants in case of evacuation

    • 2. Insurance:

  • Confirm that all participants have travel and health insurance covering outdoor activities in remote locations

    • 3. Health Precautions:

  • Check for any specific health advisories for the Nunavut region

    • V. Itinerary:

    A. Day-to-Day Schedule:

    1. Day 1 (June 1, 2024): Arrival and Orientation

  • Arrive in Iqaluit, transfer to Pangnirtung
  • Check equipment and participate in a pre-trip orientation
  • Welcome dinner in Pangnirtung

    • 2. Day 2-8 (June 2-8, 2024): Auyuittuq National Park Trail

  • Daily trekking with specific destinations such as Summit Lake, Overlord, and Thor Peak
  • Explore cultural sites, including ancient Thule sites and Inuit rock markings
  • Camping under the Arctic sky, experiencing the unique daylight of the region

    • 3. Day 9 (June 9, 2024): Return Journey

  • Trek back to Pangnirtung
  • Closing ceremony and cultural exchange
  • Final night in Pangnirtung

    • 4. Day 10 (June 10, 2024): Departure

  • Morning free time
  • Transfer to Iqaluit for departure
